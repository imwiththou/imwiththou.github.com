Some Basic Properties of Biomaterials

Bulk Properties

These properteis include mecanical properteis (strength, stiffness), physical properties (crystallinity and thermal transition) and chemical compositions.

Degradation

Degradation of biomaterials is of course concerned, no matter it's desired or undesired. The degradation of materials, macroscopically and microscopically, is largely related to the implatation.

Surface Prioperties

As water being the main composition of human body, hydrophilicity is a critical property for biomaterials ---- whether being "water-loving" (hydrophilic) or "water-fearing" (hydropholic). The surface chemical composition determines such properties.

As we mentioned at the right beginning, structure, properties and application are three aspects that's being highly connected. Structure can be interpreted from compositions, properties can be reflected by structures and using knowledge on different propetrties we may find suitable applications of those materials.

The properties can be determined/measured by characterisation. Medical field is a high risk place to step into, not like other engineering subjects, it's about lives, thouands of millions of lives. If one medical devices have lethal flaws and not being noticed during the long-long (at least 15 years) trails period, after deploying into the commercial market, patients will be in direct danger and it will cause huge trauma to them, including their relatives. Everyone involoved in the developing of the devices will be blamed, from both legal and moral sides.

Biocompatibility

Clearly, biocompatibility is highly connected with the properties of biomaterials. It reveals the properties and the properties, in contrast, determines its biocompatibility.

For an implatation or device failure, the causings may be one of the these aspects:

  1. materials

  2. design defects

  3. surgery

  4. infection

  5. patient issues

VSEPR (Valance Shell Electron Pair Repulsion) theory was introduced in the course BG1103 BioChemistry last year, which is pretty useful in predicting the molecular structure of species.

Bonding Force and Energy

Three types:

  • Ionic: depends on electronegativity, bonded by Columb force, non-directional.

  • Metallic: involving electron sharing, non-directional.

  • Covalent: through the shared valence electrons, directional.

A Bit More on Defeats on Ceramics

Defeats should not affect the whole electroneutrability of material.

There are two defeats that commonly noticed in ceramics:

  • Schottky defeats: lack of same number of cations and anions.

  • Frenkel defeats: same number of cations and anions mispalced in crystal structure as institial defeats.

Either defeat type remains the overall electroneutrability.

The introduced impurities should be the same, does not affect the electroneutrability. Commonly, cations form substitutional solutions and anions can form either substitutional or intersitital solutions. the solute ions should be similar in size and charge to the solvent ions.




Physical & Chemical Properties of Ceramics
-------


Ceramics are usually hard but brittle, that means they are easy to break under certain circumstances. They are usually good insulators, both electrically and thermally. Microscopically, stress concentrates at small imperfections will lead to sudden attack propagation, causing mechanical failures. Most ceramics are oxides, carbides or nitrides. They are chemicall inert and nonreactive to outter environments. They have very high melting points.






Physical & Chemical Properties of Metals
-------


Metals are usually strong, hard and tough. They have high strength-to-weight ratio, i.e., they can be easily strenthened out (mealleable, ductile). They have high melting points and large density. They are highly interactive with air, water or acids.
