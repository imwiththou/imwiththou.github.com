What is the difference between chilled water and cooling water?

Both provide cooling function.

Process cooling water is used for the cooling purpose of process/manufacturing tools (seperate loop with filtration).

Chilled water is water that's deliberately made colder, often with mechanical refrigerator (chiller) systems, to be used in some part of an industrial system. That system could be a chemical process, drinking water, or part of a building HVAC system for local temperature adjustment, for a few examples.

Cooling water is water used as a heat-transfer medium to carry heat away from one or more parts of a system. Sometimes chilled water is used as cooling water, but it's generally cheaper to use water starting at ambient temperature. These design considerations depend on the amount of heat that needs to be moved, the temperature rise (?T) that can be tolerated, and the mass flow of water that can be moved through the system. Starting with chilled water can increase the available ?T.

What is the difference between bulk gases and specialty gases?

Bulk gases refer to those gases are usually inert and non-hazardous in industry for ultility suppling purposes, such as Nitrogen, Oxygen, Helium, Hydrogen and Argon.

Specialty gases are usually divided into four groups by their properties:

  • Corrosive

  • Inert/toxic

  • Flammable/toxic

  • BSGS (Bulk Specialty Gas Supply system)

What is special about high purity piping?

They have to be very clean and inert to garantee that it won't generate any particle due to corrosive reaction happening between medium and piping internal surface, in another word, the medium cannot 'eat' the pipe and generate any particle, otherwise, those particles will be brought into process and block any filtration facilities, making the whole system down.

Usually the particle size limit is less than 10 nanometers in diameter.

What is water balance?

What's the difference between level 1, level 2 and level 3 schedules?

There are in total 6 levels in project planning.

L0: One-line planning, decides 'how long the project will be and gives a general Gantt chart'

L1: answer 'how many building/warehouse/chamber will be inside this project?'

L2: answer 'what's inside the building?', provides different section and components of the building utilities.

L3: answer 'how is the building going to be built?', also provides safety equipments layouts and piping, packages are planned and scheduled during this stage.

L4: Design in details, wrt each level and room.

L5: 'Micro-schedule' stage, finds problems in details (e.g. where should this nail be nailed), lights and lines are also scheduled in this stage.
